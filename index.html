Batman: Arkham

Batman: Arkham is a video game series and an action film starring the fictional superhero Batman. The first video game of this franchise was Batman: Arkham Asylum, whose story took place in the Arkham asylum, after the success of this game several sequels were released and to know that it was the same series as Arkham Asylum the pseudonym was added Arkham. The success of the games has led to the launch of several spin-offs, films and sequels in comics or other media, the best known is the comic Batman: Arkham City, which serves as a sequel to the homonymous video game. The first two games were developed by Rocksteady Studios, the third by NetherRealm Studios, and the last two by Warner Bros. Games Montréal and Armature Studios. In 2014 the animated film Batman: Assault on Arkham was released with the suicide squad as the protagonist, which is part of the Arkhamverse. In March 2014 Batman: Arkham Knight was announced, which was developed again by Rocksteady Studios for the next generation consoles, and was also confirmed as the last game in the series.
